**Document version:** 1.0  
**Date:** June 22, 2026

---

## 1. Background

Skeepon is a precision fertigation management platform designed to help commercial growers optimize nutrient delivery through their irrigation systems. The California-specific application ("sk-ca") focuses on high-value crops grown under drip and micro-irrigation regimes, where fertigation timing and nutrient ratios directly impact yield, quality, and water-use efficiency.

This PoC engagement is intended to:

- Validate that Skeepon's core fertigation logic delivers measurable agronomic and economic outcomes under California field conditions.
- Identify the most viable path to commercial adoption within the California grower market.
- Produce the business-level documentation and commercialization framework needed to support fundraising, partnership, or direct sales efforts.

## 3. Scope

The PoC is organized into three workstreams. Consulting engagements may cover all three or a defined subset as agreed upon at the start of each engagement.

### 3.1 Workstream A — Fertigation Platform Validation

**Purpose:** Confirm the platform's technical and agronomic functionality under real or simulated California field conditions.

**In scope:**
- Review and refinement of the existing Skeepon Field Fertigation Calculator (crop-specific nutrient rates, tank mix logic, injector rate calculations).
- Selection of 1–3 representative California crop types (e.g., strawberries, wine grapes, almonds) for PoC testing scenarios.
- Definition of input data requirements: field size, soil type, crop growth stage, water source EC/pH, target fertilizer formulations.
- Output validation: verify that calculator outputs align with published UC Cooperative Extension guidelines and grower-accepted practice.
- Identification of gaps between current tool capabilities and what commercial growers require.

**Out of scope:**
- Physical on-farm installation or sensor hardware.
- Integration with third-party farm management software (addressed in a future phase).

**Deliverables:**
- Agronomic validation report comparing Skeepon outputs to reference benchmarks.
- Prioritized feature gap list with effort estimates.

---

### 3.2 Workstream B — Business Requirements

**Purpose:** Capture what growers, agronomists, and retailers need from the platform in order to adopt and pay for it.

**In scope:**
- Stakeholder interviews: 5–10 interviews with California growers, PCAs (Pest Control Advisers), crop consultants, and ag retailers.
- Identification of key user personas and their primary jobs-to-be-done.
- Documentation of current workflows consultants and growers use for fertigation management.
- Requirements gathering covering: ease of use, data ownership, regulatory compliance (CDFA, Water Board), reporting formats, mobile access.
- Competitive landscape summary: how Skeepon differentiates from alternatives (spreadsheets, existing ag-software, distributor recommendations).

**Deliverables:**
- User persona profiles (3–5 personas).
- Business requirements document (BRD) covering functional and non-functional requirements.
- Competitive positioning one-pager.

---

### 3.3 Workstream C — Commercialization Pathway

**Purpose:** Define the steps needed to move Skeepon from a validated PoC to a marketable, revenue-generating product in California.

**In scope:**
- Market sizing: estimate the addressable California irrigated acreage relevant to Skeepon's feature set.
- Go-to-market channel analysis: direct-to-grower, agronomist/PCA channel, retail/distributor partnerships, co-marketing with fertilizer brands.
- Pricing model options: per-acre SaaS, subscription tiers, freemium-to-premium, consulting-bundled licensing.
- Regulatory and certification considerations specific to California (Fertilizer Research & Education Program, water board compliance, data privacy).
- Partnership opportunity map: identify 5–10 potential early adopter accounts or channel partners.
- Risk assessment: agronomic liability, data accuracy, adoption barriers, competitive response.

**Deliverables:**
- Commercialization roadmap document with phased milestones (PoC → Pilot → Early Revenue → Scale).
- Go-to-market strategy brief.
- Partnership target list with outreach recommendations.
- Pricing model recommendation with assumptions and sensitivities.

## 5. Assumptions and Constraints

- Consultants will be provided access to the existing Skeepon fertigation calculator codebase and any internal agronomic reference data available.
- Grower interview recruitment will be coordinated by AC Planta; consultants will conduct the interviews.
- This PoC does not include software development beyond documentation of requirements; any product changes identified will be scoped separately.
- All work is subject to a mutual NDA between AC Planta and the consulting partner.

## 8. Success Criteria

The PoC is considered successful when:

1. The fertigation calculator outputs are validated against at least one California crop with documented agronomic rationale.
2. A business requirements document is accepted by AC Planta as an accurate representation of target user needs.
3. A commercialization roadmap is delivered with at least three distinct go-to-market pathways evaluated and a recommended path identified.
4. AC Planta has sufficient documentation to brief investors, partners, or a product development team on the next phase.

---

## 9. Out of Scope (for this PoC)

- Hardware procurement, sensor installation, or on-farm physical trials.
- Software engineering or product development.
- Legal entity formation, fundraising materials, or investor pitch decks (these may be follow-on engagements).
- Support for crops or geographies outside California for this engagement.
